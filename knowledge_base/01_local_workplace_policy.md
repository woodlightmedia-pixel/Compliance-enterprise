# DOCUMENT 01: INTERNAL WORKPLACE POLICY – DIGNITY AT WORK

## 1. Statement of Intent
This organization operates a zero-tolerance policy regarding bullying, harassment, victimization, and discrimination of any kind. Every employee, contractor, and client has the right to be treated with dignity, fairness, and mutual respect. 

## 2. Prohibited Behaviors
* **Workplace Bullying:** Persistent, offensive, intimidating, malicious, or insulting behavior, or an abuse of power intended to undermine, humiliate, or cause physical or emotional harm.
* **Harassment:** Unwanted conduct that violates an individual's dignity or creates an intimidating, hostile, degrading, humiliating, or offensive environment.
* **Discrimination:** Treating an individual less favorably based on personal or cultural identity traits (direct), or implementing blanket policies that unfairly disadvantage specific groups without legitimate justification (indirect).

## 3. Racial & Equality Commitments
* Explicit prohibition of racial discrimination, including overt slurs, microaggressions, stereotyping, and code-switching pressures.
* Equal access to promotion, training, and operational projects across all demographics.

## 4. Internal Reporting & Grievance Procedures
If an employee experiences or witnesses prohibited conduct, they should follow this sequential pathway:
1. **Informal Resolution (Optional):** If safe and appropriate, clearly state to the perpetrator that the behavior is unwelcome and must cease.
2. **Formal Reporting:** Lodge a formal written grievance to the Human Resources (HR) Department or an designated alternate internal Line Manager.
3. **Investigation:** HR will appoint an impartial investigator. Timelines demand initial assessment within 5 working days and full resolution within 28 working days.
4. **Protection Against Reprisal:** Victimization or retaliation against any employee who raises a complaint in good faith is a gross misconduct disciplinary offense.
